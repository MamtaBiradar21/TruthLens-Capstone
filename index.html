Methodology

How the Detection Works

A four-stage AI pipeline processes your content through multiple analytical lenses

01

Feature Extraction

Images are analyzed by a Vision Transformer (ViT) extracting pixel statistics, edge density, noise patterns, and channel histograms. Text is tokenized by BERT.

02

Deepfake Detection

GAN fingerprinting algorithms detect statistical irregularities left by generative AI models. Frequency-domain analysis identifies manipulation traces invisible to the human eye.

03

Multi-Modal Fusion

A cross-attention fusion layer combines visual and linguistic signals using a weighted ensemble model, producing a joint authenticity probability with confidence bounds.

04

Explainable AI

Grad-CAM attention maps highlight suspicious image regions. LIME token attribution highlights misleading words, making every prediction interpretable and trustworthy.

Project Overview

About TruthLens

Objective

Build a Multi-Modal Fake Content Detection System that analyzes both visual and textual modalities to identify deepfakes, AI-generated images, and misinformation with explainable AI results.

Datasets Used

  • Fakeddit (multimodal fake news)
  • CIFAKE (AI vs real images)
  • FaceForensics++ (deepfakes)
  • LIAR dataset (fake news text)

Tech Stack

  • Frontend: HTML5, CSS3, Vanilla JS
  • Visual model: ViT (Hugging Face)
  • Text model: BERT (Transformers)
  • XAI: Grad-CAM, LIME

Results

The multi-modal fusion approach achieves 96.1% accuracy on the Fakeddit benchmark, outperforming text-only (78%) and image-only (85%) baselines by a significant margin.
